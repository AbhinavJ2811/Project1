Here is the time line of Cormac McCarthy:

  • 1933: Cormac McCarthy is born on July 20 in Providence, Rhode Island, USA.
  • 1965: "The Orchard Keeper" - McCarthy's debut novel is published. It receives critical acclaim.
  • 1973: "Child of God" - McCarthy publishes this novel, which explores themes of isolation and violence in rural Tennessee.
  • 1979: "Suttree" - McCarthy releases this novel, which is set in Knoxville, Tennessee, and is widely regarded as one of his major works.
  • 1985: "Blood Meridian, or the Evening Redness in the West" - Considered one of McCarthy's masterpieces, this novel is published. It is a brutal and philosophical exploration of violence set in the American Southwest in the mid-19th century.
  • 1992: "All the Pretty Horses" - McCarthy publishes the first installment in his Border Trilogy, which also includes "The Crossing" (1994) and "Cities of the Plain" (1998). "All the Pretty Horses" wins the National Book Award for Fiction.
  • 2005: "No Country for Old Men" - This novel, set along the Texas-Mexico border, is published and later adapted into an Academy Award-winning film directed by Joel and Ethan Coen.
  • 2006: McCarthy's novel "The Road" is published. It depicts a post-apocalyptic world and follows the journey of a father and his son. It wins the Pulitzer Prize for Fiction.
  • 2013: "The Counselor" - McCarthy writes the screenplay for this film directed by Ridley Scott.
  • 2019: "The Passenger" - McCarthy publishes this novel, which tells the story of a young man who becomes involved in illegal activities on the Texas-Mexico border.
  • 2023 Dies at the age of 89
